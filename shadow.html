01 / Introduction

Shadow is an adventurous and puzzle game, aiming to help people cultivate a new way of thinking. In this game, players need to constantly shift their perspectives on the barriers and passages based on the mechanic of the game, black and white terrains reversing, in order to find the right ways to destinations. Apart from good judgment on terrains, players also need to choose the opportune moments to move, as well as to avoid monsters and other obstacles.

Dual World Maze
The game is based on parallel universes, the white world and black world. There are no absolute passages or barriers. For instance, in white world, there is a wall blocking you from passing through. However, it becomes a passage when you press the reversing button and get into the black world.

Adventurous Elements
We introduced thorns, monsters, lightning, switch and blockage to excite players, making it more entertaining and engaging.

02 / Ideation

“ In many cases people are bustling around to solve the problems, addicted to their rigid thinking, while they seldom stop and move outside of it to seek an innovative or alternative solution. ”

At the very beginning, we all determined to create an innovative and meaningful game that is able to inspire people to cultivate a new way of thinking or provoke their certain feelings, rather than mere replication of popular games in the Top Charts. We were exploring what our game is going to be and what it could be. During the brainstorm, we found a common problem that occurs in our daily life, while many people cannot overcome or even notice it.

Based on this, the theme of our game was designed to be:

“ Another sight, another world. ”

Inspiration

Inspired by M. C. Escher and his illusion art works, we were thinking of creating parallel universes that can be changed into each other from different perspectives in our game. To make players stop for a while and reflect on the problems from different points of view, the genre of puzzle games was selected to obtain this goal.

 

03 / Level Design

There are 4 fantastic chapters in the game, including 8 levels in each chapter. Each chapter has a unique gameplay mechanism and chapter skill.

Players are required to:
1. have good judgment on terrains
2. have spatial imagination


3. seize the opportunity to move
4. take advantages of chapter skills

Chapter 1
Black & White Reversing

Players use basic reversing function to switch between black and white worlds to find a right way

Chapter 2
Gravity Flip

Players have ten seconds to change the direction of gravity once they get reversing items.

Chapter 3
Modify Terrains

Players are able to use limited cubes to build a suitable route to destination and avoid being hurt.

Chapter 4
Replication

Players are able to summon a replicated self to turn on the switches or control them to areas that cannot be reached by players.

Sketch

I sketched rudimental ideas in papers and checked if these levels worked. In order to make sure there is only one way to destination, I placed obstacles in different positions to block out other possible ways to destination. Additionally, to make routes more tortuous and create exciting experiences, I created a situation allowing players get through a passage only with the help of certain items that needed to be obtained somewhere else.

Standardized Design of Game levels

I designed game levels in Excel since square units in Excel make designing standardized levels easier. A game level is typically represented by 29*29 units in a sheet of excel. I determined the amount of units that every element takes and the positions of a variety of adventurous elements. Different elements are represented by different colors in Excel.
There are some benefits of designing levels in Excel:
1. Clear guideline for the programmer to construct the game levels.
2. Unified visual elements.
3. Clear reflection of real game level in scale of Excel.

Sustainability

Since designing and testing a game level consumes a lot of time and energy, I need to find out a sustainable way to enlarge the amounts of game levels. I designed a mechanic, the Edit Mode, using rewards to encourage players to design their unique game levels.Players are able to share their own levels and play the levels designed by others on our game platform. Our system will automatically calculate each level’s success rate of defense based on players' records. The higher the level’s success rate of defense, the more rewards players will get once they pass the level. Vice versa, the game players who design levels with higher success rate of defense also get more rewards.

04 / Graphic Design & Animation

Black-and-White Silhouetted Style

1. Black-and-white style to form a sharp contrast
2. Construct terrains with rounded rectangle
3. Cute character and monsters

Flat User Interface

1. Black-and-white tones
2. Concise UI
3. Clear metaphors

Art Style Iteration

To improve quality, we went through two main art style iterations. The art style of the first version stressed the outlines of objects. While we found the visual effects presented in mobile devices were not satisfactory. Additionally, the detailed style made our game's visual impression overcomplicated. Finally, we decided to make the style more concise and impactful, thus black-and-white silhouetted style was created.

Animation

I first sketched the animation sequence on papers to learn the features of characters’ movements. After figuring out how the character moves and determining several key frames of the movement, I used Photoshop to create a series of postures by moving the body components in different angles and positions. Finishing all motion frames, I adjusted the intervals to make it a smooth movement.
