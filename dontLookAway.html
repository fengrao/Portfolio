Introduction

Don't look away is a PC game whose mechanics are based on eye gaze, keyboard and mouse input. In the game, players need to finish the pre-interview quiz questions during the Skype interview, while not be found by the interviewer.

Design

1. Intuitive Interactions


    -  Eye gaze as an input:
        We consider eye gaze as one of the most intuitive interaction. We rely on it almost every second and use it to tell our brains the information of surrounding environment. If we can use eye gaze as an input to interact with objects or trigger mechanics, it will be very efficient and intuitive.

    -  Mouse and keyboard
        The mouse and keyboard are very commonly used as game controllers. However, the mouse and keyboard are not necessarily the most intuitive interaction between player and games. We want to simulate a daily situation where they use mouse and keyboard for work rather than as game controllers.

2. Clues and Feedbacks in The Game


    -  When the interviewer is about to raise his head
        To give the player an indication of dangers, we use audio clues and interviewer raising head animation to indicate that the interviewer is going to look at the player. The player should quickly move his eye gaze and makes eye contact with the interviewer when they notice these feedbacks.
    -  When not looking at the interviewer
        The interviewer will look down and read the resume. In this time, players can take this opportunity to finish the questionnaires.

3. Immersive Design


    -  Game mechanics
    We carefully designed the game mechanics to make it engaging. We provided a goal for player to accomplish while pose some limitation, such as interviewer will suddenly raise his head, making eye contacts with the interviewee.

    -  Daily life simulation
    The Skype interview is an familiar scene in people's daily life.

Challenges

The eye calibration is less than perfect.


    -  Normal People:
        For normal people, the eye gaze in the screen will lower than where the player is exactly looking at. The player have to raise their head in order to make acceptable eye contact.

    -  People with glasses
        Eye Gaze Tracker is not friendly to people with glasses. The player's gaze will lose track when wearing glasses. They need to take off the glasses to finish the calibration. While without glasses, they may not see clearly the contents on the screen, which would be a bad experience.
