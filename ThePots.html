The wait is an emotionally immersive AR storytelling experience using Microsoft Hololens, in which players can engage in the story of a mother and her sick daughter by interacting with physical objects and virtual characters.

Interactions in AR

1. Interacting with physical objects


To make players immerse in the story, we want them to interact with real world objects to trigger the memories in the story. So we designed several physical objects as our interfaces.

2. Interacting with virtual characters


We also want players to participate in the story, rather than just being audiences watching the story. Thus, we designed several mechanisms to allow players to physically interact with virtual characters.

1) Hand the paper to the little girl.
Players are asked to hand the little girl a real world object - paper, to the little girl. When players hand the paper to her, the little girl will start drawing a butterfly the same as it appears in the paper. This will give player a sense that they are parts of the story, participating the event in the memories.

2) Give the little girl a hug.
When the little girl is seriously ill and feels very cold, she will ask players to give her a hug. When players approach and hug her, she will evaporate into small pieces, which will make players feel the memory is fragile and temporary.
