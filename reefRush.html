Introduction


Reef Rush is a mobile RPG game, in which the player makes the selected fish to grow up by eating smaller fish and avoiding big fish. In addition, there are a variety of fishes with different skills, which can be unlocked and upgraded during the gameplay. I was responsible for designing and implementing UI motions/animations with Adobe Flash for this game of its early stage.

Motion/ Animation Design

Early Prototype Of Modes Selection

This is the Modes selection UI flow prototype for the game. Different modes were represented by different planets. When players choose the button on the right, the planets will swipe down and new one will raise up. I also designed some subtle motions for this animation. When a new planet raises up, it will bounce back a little bit. Additionally, the time that different planets swipe down and the speed of their movements are different based on the distances from planets to viewer.

Chapters Selection

I designed and implemented the transition between Main Interface and Chapters Selection Interface. For buttons in the same hierarchy, the time of swiping back and appearing for each button are different. Once players select the "Play" button, it will go to Chapters Selection Interface. In this interface, players are able to choose one chapter by tapping one of the lands, or change the modes on the left.

Levels, Characters and Assisting Friends Selection UI/UX Flow

This shows the UI/UX flow of selecting level and character. There is a route in the Level Interface. When players tap a level, it will go to Level Detail Interface. Players can see the description, requirements of this level on the left. Players also can see the detail of the character and change characters on the right by swiping left and right. Once players tap the orange button at the bottom of the left, it will go to Friends Interface, in which players can select a friend to assist them finish the level.

Characters' Skills Upgrading UI/UX Flow First Version

This is the first version of Characters' Skills Upgrading UI/UX Flow. Players can tap the skill icons on the left side, each icon represents one skill that character has. The panel on the right shows the detail of skills, including skill icon, description, cost of upgrading and an orange "Upgrade" button. The icon on the top of left side shows all skills that current character has. Players can change characters on the character navigation bar in the bottom. For this version, we only showed one skill detail each time(panel on the right). So the upgrading skill interface is clear to player. However, the efficiency of upgrading skills could be a little lower than second version. Since players have to change to other skill, and then tap "Upgrade" button. This takes two steps.

Characters' Skills Upgrading UI/UX Flow Second Version

This is the second version of Characters' Skills Upgrading UI/UX Flow. Most elements are the same as the first version excepting the panel on the right. This time I showed several skills on the panel and highlight the selected one. If the skill can be upgraded, there will be a green "+" upgrade button. If the skill is upgrade to maximum level, then the upgrade button will become gray mark. While if the skill haven't been unlocked, then there will be a lock icon on it. For this version, the efficiency of upgrading skills is very high. Players can simply swipe up and down or just tap the "Upgrade" button to upgrade. However, this version of interface does not show too much detail of skills. Additionally, the information will be overwhelming to players.
