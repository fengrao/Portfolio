Introduction


SYSU Wings is an application aiming to provide birds observers with useful information about local birds and help them quickly identify birds they observed based on birdsâ€™ features and current seasons.

Background

Background

1. Guangzhou lies in the intersection of two migration routes of East Asia-Australia and West Pacific Ocean, which makes it a perfect place for bird observation.
2. Currently, few applications for bird observation are available in App Store and other markets.
3. Bird-observation novices are unable to quickly identify birds they observed. Additionally, using traditional paper books is so inefficient that birds have flew away before beginners find them out in books.

Design Goal


1. Provide comprehensive guidelines for bird observation in order to help people get full understanding of local birds.
2. Assist users to efficiently identify birds they observed based on current migration seasons and songs of birds.

Target Users


Bird-observation novices in Guangzhou.

User Interview

To better understand the scenario and problems of bird observation, we conducted user interview and consulted the specialists in bird observation for more information.

Key Questions to Be Explored:

1. What is the distribution of birds during migration season in certain areas? Is it distinctive enough?
2. Based on which features can observers identify a bird?
3. What is the scenario of bird observation?
4. What are advantages and disadvantages of traditional guidelines?
5. What kinds of information about bird species do observers want to know?

Nuoya Tao is a student in Sun Yat-sen University. He also is a president of an NGO dedicating to bird observation and conservation. Though he majors in Clinical Medicine, he grasps solid knowledge of biology, especially of birds. He usually organizes his teammates to observe birds outdoors on weekends. He found that some novices couldn't quickly identify certain bird species due to lack of experience. At the same time, the traditional paper guidelines for bird observation provide little help since comparing birds they seen and those in books is low-efficient.

Interview Key Points

We gained some key information that helps us provide useful and usable solutions based on their needs and scenario.

Feature

Users identify a bird based on its head, mouth, plumage, songs and calls, tails etc.

Time

They observe birds primarily in spring, autumn, winter. Specifically, three hours before sunrise and three hours after sunset.

Location

To select a suitable location for observation, users should take topography, plantation, seasons, species of birds and other issues into consideration holistically.

Weather

The appropriate weather for observation is a sunny day or a sunny day after rainfalls since sunlight is sufficient at this time.

Observation Habit

Users prefer to observe birds with their peers.

Distribution

Distribution is not limited to specific environment or areas. It ranges a large scale.

How to use Guidelines

Users need a thorough preview of the paper guidelines before observation. Typically, most users search for a specific bird in reference book based on their memories.

Paper Guidelines

They systematically introduce bird species with details and vivid pictures. While they make birds searching low efficient. Besides, they are too heavy to carry around.

Problem Definition

Comprehensive knowledge of birds

SYSU Wings provides users with useful information of birds, including picture, habit, feature, visibility, endangered category and length of body.

Very low efficiency in birds searching

Based on seasons of bird migration, the APP provides information of possible bird species, according to which users identify birds.

Indirect understanding on size of birds

We used visualization methods to make a clear and direct comparison between length of birds and human palms.

Lack of joy when observing birds with peers

We designed an illustration mode in which users can record what they observed within a setting time. They can compare records with their peers.
