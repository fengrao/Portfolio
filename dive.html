Brief

Vive is a VR game in HTC VIVE. Player's goal is to gather certain amount of fish by guiding them and blinding sharks.

- Platform:   HTC VIVE
- Time:   Sept. 2016 (2 weeks)

- Role:   UX engineer, Artist
- Tools:   C#, Maya

Reflection on UX in VR

1. HUD Is Not Wise Choice In VR.


    -  Effective:
        The condition for the alert to appear is that there is a shark in the world. While the sharks appear too frequently, sharks almost never die out. So the HUD shark alert does not help the player to tell whether sharks appear.
        The HUD does not indicate the direction of the sharks. It only provides if the shark present in the world. Players cannot know where the sharks are. The frequently appearing alert makes players feel neveous, but it cannot provide useful information to them, making a frustrating experience.

    -  Satisfactory:
        The frequently appearing alerts hinders the players to appreciate the beautiful underwater.

2. Use Diegetic Clues As Interfaces.


    -  Fish Direction:
    We can use fishes direction to indicate where the sharks are. The environment elements in the game can function as indicators. It will provide a immersive experience while providing useful information to players.

    -  Number of Fish Information:
    The number of the fish information can be places in the players hand. This will not hinder players appreciating the beautiful scene. If players want to know the information, they can just raise their hands.
